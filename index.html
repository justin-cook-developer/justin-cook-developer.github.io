Get to Know Me: My Journey to Fullstack at SLO

First things first, why coding?

I'll come right out and say it, I love to code. I thoroughly enjoy the analytical thinking that coding requires, the grind of fixing bugs, and the sweet success of a working program. My passion for the act of coding itself is complemented by my desire to create something impactful, and valuable to society. Thus, web programming is the perfect medium with which to make my mark, and is a career in which I know I can thrive! Now that you have an idea of my motives for attending Fullstack at SLO, I'll back track and explain how I've gotten to where I am today.

Why me?

Now that you know how I ended up pursuing Fullstack at SLO, I'd like you to know why I would be the best representative for Ticket Into Tech's alternative pathway for recent high school graduates.


First and foremost, I am extremely passionate about coding. Passion is a paramount component of excelling at any activity, and I have the requisite passion to keep learning and working hard. Furthermore, I have cultivated an extremely strong work ethic and the self-discipline to complement it. I am rarely the smartest person in the room, but I make up for any innate disadvantages by working harder than anybody else. I am hungry for success, and I have no problem putting in the hours to achieve success. This has served me very well in my academics and extracurriculars, and I believe it will also serve me well in a professional setting. In addition, I am also an effective collaborator. I have been a part of many successful teams, and I have even lead a few successful projects myself, such as the sustainable seafood video above.


In closing, I believe I have the necessary aptitudes to be a successful software engineer and I will try my darndest to show that tech apprenticeships are a viable, alternative path to success.
